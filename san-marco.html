Serving San Marco With the Care It Deserves

San Marco is one of Jacksonville's most distinctive and admired neighborhoods — an upscale historic district with a personality unlike anywhere else in the city. The iconic lions that guard the San Marco Square fountain have welcomed residents and visitors for decades, and the Mediterranean Revival architecture that gives the neighborhood its character makes it one of the most visually striking places in all of Northeast Florida.

San Marco Blvd and the surrounding streets host an eclectic mix of boutique shops, acclaimed restaurants, galleries, and professional offices — all wrapped in an architectural style that feels more like an Italian piazza than a Florida neighborhood. The residential streets branching off from the commercial center are lined with stately homes, garden apartments, and historic buildings that represent some of Jacksonville's most valued real estate.

For a locksmith, San Marco presents a genuinely interesting challenge — and one we welcome. Historic homes and buildings in this neighborhood often have original lock hardware, mortise lock sets, ornate door hardware, and older security systems that require knowledge and care. At North Florida Locksmith, we've worked on all of it. We know how to handle older hardware with the respect it deserves, and we know how to integrate modern security upgrades in a way that doesn't compromise the historic character of a property.

Historic Home Lock Expertise in San Marco

San Marco's residential streets are filled with properties that were built in the 1920s, 1930s, and 1940s, and many of them have original door hardware that has never been replaced. Mortise locks, skeleton key systems, antique brass hardware — these are a regular part of what we encounter in San Marco, and we handle them with care.

We won't tell you to simply rip out your original mortise hardware and replace it with a modern deadbolt if that's not the right call for your home. In many cases, we can service the existing hardware, replace worn internal components, and recut keys for the original system. When a genuine upgrade is needed, we'll help you find a high-quality option that respects the aesthetic of your door and your home.

At the same time, many San Marco residents want to add modern security features — keyless entry, smart locks, or upgraded deadbolts — without compromising the appearance of their historic home. We've done this type of installation many times, and we approach it thoughtfully. There are excellent lock options on the market today that look at home on a period-appropriate door while providing substantially better security than what was originally installed.
